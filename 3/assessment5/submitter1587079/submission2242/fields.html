Because of this high transcription rate hey are usually kept free of methylation independent of their activity state. Healthy cells can usually be distinguished from cancer cells promoter because CpG islands in cancer cells tend to become hypermethylated, which then causes silencing of the underlying gene. Intragenic methylation is found at repetitive sequences such as satellite repeats and remnants of retroviral insertions such as LINEs and SINEs in human DNA. Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine is a DNA-demethylating agent, used to treat myelodysplastic syndromes, the precursors of acute myelogenous leukaemia. The anti-tumour effect is based on demethylation of DNA, so epigenetic changes that are passed on during cell division to daughter and granddaughter cells are actively erased. Once they are erased, they do not return which is most welcome because by using epigenetic therapies, changes can be made which stop division and growth of cancer cells without killing its, as well as the neighbouring, healthy cells.
