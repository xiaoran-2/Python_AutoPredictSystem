Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

                       

DNA methylation involves the addition of a methyl residues to DNA which is a modification of cytosine by adding methl group (CH3) to the number 5 carbon of the cyosine pyrimidine ring.  This modification can be inherited through cell divison (mitosis) and occurs almost at CpG dinucleotides in mammals. P refer to phosphodiester bond between cytosine and guanine nucleotides.  The regions of the genome that have a higher concentration of CpG sites are known as CpG islands.  Normal DNA methylation occurs primarily at two developmental stages: early embryogenesis & gametogenesis.  Somehow epigenetic alteration occurs which alter the heritable state of gene expression by fomation of transcriptionally repressive chromatin states around gene transcription sites and gain of methylation in normally unmethylated CpG islands in these regions.  The aberrant methylation of gene promoter regions begin very early in tumor progression, affect over half of the tumor suppressor genes can lead to cancer. It has a long range impact on the phenotype without altering the genotype.  These gene promoter CpG islands acquire abnormal hypermethylation which results in trascriptional silencing  that can be inherited by daughter cells following cell division. In normal cell, we can find hypomethylation in the CpGisland region but the genome is methylated in the repetitive elements through the intergenic regions and the intron of gene.  In contrast, the cancer cell more likely to have hypermethylation or just methylated in the CpG island region but the repetitive elements, the intergenic region and the intron of gene are hypomethylated.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

                       

With Imprinted genes, we inherit only one working copy from either paternal or maternal alleles while the other is imprinted or silenced.  Since most of imprinted gene we found are involved in growth,  either promoting or suppressing. With some kind of abnormal imprinted control can lead to cancer. Let's consider Igf2/H19 cluster from human chromosome11.  In a normal maternal allele, CTCF insulator protein insulates Igf2 growth factor from downstream enhancer which enhance H19 expression and suppress Igf2 factor while on the other hand, we have methylated paternal allele on the imprinted control region of the cluster where CTCF no longer binded.  Without an insulator protein, enhancers are free to act on Igf2  which lead to Igf2 growth factor expression on the paternal allele.  Igf2 is a growth promoter in this case possible oncogene.  However, with the loss of imprinting control, you have hypamethylation of the imprint control region in both parental allele and it lead to expression of Igf2 on maternal allele as well.  Now you have double dose of Igf2 which is growth promoting and it is associated with Wilm's tumor.

Decitabine is a class of a drugs known as DNA demethylating agent.  Methylation of DNA is a major mechanism that regulates gene expression in cells.  It hypomethylates DNA by inhibiting DNA methyltransferase. It is indicated for the treatment of myelodysplastic syndromes.  Decitabine anticancer effects work in 2 ways.  First  it works by demethlation or interfering with the methylation of DNA, this will restore the normal function of tumor suppressor genes and gaining the control of cell growth.  Secondly, it belongs to the category of chemotherapy called antimetabolites. When cell incorporate Decitabine into cellular metabolism, it interacts with number of targets within the cell to produce a direct cytotoxic effect that causes death of rapidly dividing cancer cells.
