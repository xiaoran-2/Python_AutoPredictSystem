Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation is a stable and inactive epigenetic mark which occurs exclusively at CpG islands and is maintained by DNMT1 which recognises hemi methylated DNA strand and lays down methylation on new daughter strand.Since it is copied by daughter strand at every cell division it is considered to be a stable epigenetic mark. Methylation of CpG islands is associated with formation of repressive chromatin structures , tend to prohibit transcriptional factor binding and alter gene expression .Hence CpG islands tend to be protected from methylation which leads to silencing of gene expression.Eg Inactivation of Xchromosome  which is visible in female calico cats coat color( mottled).In Cancer there is activation of oncogenes and Inactivation of tumor suppressor genes. Cancer cells undergo Hyper methylation at CpG islands instead of hypo methylation.There is loss of imprinting at ICR .DNA hyper methylation is reversible.Eg MLHI in Colorectal Cancer.

Intergenic regions or Repetitive elements are generally methylated in normal cells but opposite occurs in cancer cells which leads to genome wide hypo methylation.It is the earliest found epigenetic abnormality.Hypomethylation depends on the location.It generally leads to oncogene activation due to poor promoters.Eg miRNA 21 activates tumor suppressor gene p TEN in Gliomas.Hypo methylation at Repeats happens early and progresses with time which leads to Genomic instability like Deletions, Reciprocal Translocations,Insertions etc

Role of DNA methylation in cancer is context dependent.Deletion of DNMT1 can enhance or suppress tumorigenesis.If tumor is driven by Tumor suppressor hypermethylation then depletion of DNA methylation appears to suppress tumorigenesis or if it is driven by chromosomal instability then depletion of DNA methylation enhances tumorigenesis.It is also stage specific i.e Early or late in tumorigenesis.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

Epigenetic reprogramming in Paternal genome is rapid and active demethylation as it is done via hydroxylation.In Maternal genome it is Passive demethylation as DNMT1 is excluded from nucleus.After implantation the epigenetic marks are re-established in lineage specific fashion.

Genomic immprinting is mono allelic gene expression,allele specific and tissue specific.Imprint is associated with DNA at Imprint control region( ICR).H19 is a long non coding RNA.H19/IgF2 cluster is present on human chromosome 11.

In Maternal allelewhen ICR is unmethylated ,it is bound by CTCF which is a insulator protein.It insulates IgF2 from downstream enhancers and enhances H19 to be expressed as enhancers cannot bind to IgF2.Hence IgF2 is not expressed

In Paternal allele When ICR is methylated  it blocks binding of CTCF .Hence enhancers bind to IgF2 which is expressed.Without CTCF DNA Methylation spreads to H19 promoters to silence its expression.

Upregulation of Growth promoting genes like IgF2,loss of imprimting,loss of tumor suppressor genes leads to cancer.symptoms are fetal or post natal over growth,Macroglossia,Predisposition to embryonic/childhood tumors .eg Wilm’s tumors which occurs in kidney.
