Decitabine, or 5-aza-2'-deoxycytidine is a drug used in treating cancer. Chemically, it is a cytidine analog. Decitabine is a hypomethylating agent and it acts by inhibiting DNA methyltransferase which is known to methylate tumor suppressor genes in cancer cells and maintaining this methylation. Furthermore, Decitabine is considered to be an epigenetic drug, a drug that performs epigenetic changes on tumor cells so that they are more susceptible to other drugs and mechanisms used to treat cancer, as epigenetic drugs cause inheritable changes that may have the potential to eliminate cancer without affecting the genome. Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

The two genes are part of the chromosome 7 and are regulated by two regions. Igf2 is regulated by a set of tissue-specific enhancers and H19 is regulated by a differentially methylated region. In normal cells, several sites upstream of H19 are methylated (Igf2 is expressed) on the paternal allele and unmethylated (Igf2 not expressed) on the maternal allele. When cancer exists, the maternal chromosome gets the paternal type of methylation of the H19 promoter, in which Igf2 is turned on, and H19 is turned off, causing increased cell growth and therefore cancer. Wilm's tumor is caused when the maternal copy of Igf2 is activated, while the maternal copy of H19 is silenced and this is called loss of imprinting.
