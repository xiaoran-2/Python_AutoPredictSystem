Normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands:

  • It influences various epigenetic phenomena such as imprinting, X chromosome inactivation or the formation of heterochromatin typically suppressing the gene expression.
  • CpG islands are present in about 60% of promoters of gene expression and are free of methylation.

DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer:

  • On the other hand CpG islands in cancer cells tend to become hypermethylated.

DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to disease:

  • The hyper methylation of promoter CpG islands  causes silencing of the underlying gene which may be a tummor suppressor.
  • DNA methylation is mitotically inheritable.
  • Example hypermethylation if BRCA1 in breast cancer

DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.

Intragenic methylation is found at repetitive sequences such as satellite repeats and remnants of retroviral insertions such as LINEs and SINEs in human DN. These result in  highly expressed genes, inhibit antisense transcription,repress transcriptional noise from alternate start sites and chromatin compartmentalization.

 DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer:

Directed hypomethylation at satellite repeats are found in malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors using a genome-wide approach.

DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to disease:

  • Long-range silencing of repetitive sequences is lost
  • over expression of growth promoting genes
  • inhibit antisense transcription,
  • repress transcriptional noise from alternate start sites and chromatin compartmentalization.

class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to:          

  •   Decitabine belongs to class of  DNMTi(DNA methyltransferasinhibitor) there by causing hypo methylation.

impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation: (induce antineoplastic activity via two mechanisms)

  • inhibition of DNA methyltransferase at low doses, causing hypomethylation of DNA, and
  • direct cytotoxicity in abnormal hematopoietic cells in the bone marrow through its incorporation into DNA and RNA at high doses, resulting in cell death

DNA methylation can have enduring effects on the   epigenome:

Dr. Stephen Baylin , have shown that a combination of a histone-deacetylase inhibitor and DMTi (Decitabine) slowed tumour growth in some people with advanced lung cancer and participants without much response to these  showed  good reaction to the routine chemotherapeutic drugs subsequently. This shows that epigenetic drugs altered the tumour cells in some lasting way,also more susceptible to standard chemotherapy.The lasting effects are quite possible. since epigenetic changes are passed on during cell division to daughter and granddaughter cells until they are actively erased. Once erased using the inhibitors(epigentic drugs), they do not return. Effectively epigenetic therapies can stop cancer growth without having to kill all its cells as in the case of chemotherapy.

Meaning sensitive period and the period in development:

It is the period in which epigenetic reprogramming happens.The  periods are

  • primordial germ cell development (production of mature eggs and sperms )and
  • fetal germline (preimplanted and early post implantation period) development

treating patients during sensitive periods would be inadvisable:

Since epigenetic reprogramming happens during this period, large insult to the environment would be taking a drug  that inhibits the epigenetic machinery which  may have adverse implication on the well being of the patient in the long term.
