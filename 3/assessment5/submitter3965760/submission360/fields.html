Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

CpG islands are located at the promoters of genes.In a normal cell they are hypomethylated .Methylation at CpG islands causes condensation of chromatin resulting in scilencing gene expression.

In cancer cells the CpG islands at tumor suppressor genes are increasingly methylated.

By hypermethylation cancer cells epigenetically silence the tumor suppresser genes. Since DNA methylation is mitotically heritable it forms a good way of locking the epigenetic scilencing in the cell. This methylation mediated silencing can represent one of hits in Knudson’s hypothesis. These epimutations are also rapidly selected for and over time the mutated cells divide rapidly forming a cancerous growth.

Intergenic regions and repetitive elements are usually methylated in a normal cell.

DNA methylation in intergenic regions maintains genomic integrity. Methylation also silences expression of cryptic promoters or a cryptic splice site preventing transcriptional interference which can occur if the promoter of a gene and the cryptic promoter are activated simultaneously.

DNA methylation in repetitive elements maintains genomic integrity by silencing of repeats. this prevents genomic disruption by protecting genome from transposable elements jumping around.it also prevents Transcriptional disruption by strong or cryptic promoters.                                                                                                    DNA methylation in intergenic regions prevents illegitimate recombination by keeping chromatin in compact form.

In cancer the intergenic regions and repetitive elements tend to be unmethylated and  hypomethylation occurs mostly at repetitive elements.

Cancer cells with unmethylated intergenic regions and repetitive elements exhibit genomic instability .the hypomethylation makes chromatin less compact and misalignment and subsequent illegitimate recombination  of repeats results. Hypomethylation activates the repeats and they transpose around the genome.this can disrupt or activate neighbouring genes. Hypomethylation can also result in activation of cryptic promoters and disruption of neighbouring genes. Overall this leads to additional  illegitimate deletions, insertions, duplications and reciprocal translocations  resulting in abnormal karyotype and chromosomal instability


Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

The imprint control region on the H19/Igf2 cluster is methylated on the paternal allele meaning it is paternally imprinted.The ICR methylation means CTCF can not bind to and insulate Igf2 . The enhancers are therefore free to act on Igf2 and promote Igf2 expression from the paternal allele .The methylation at ICR spreads to the H19 CpG island promoter  causing its methylation and subsequent inactivation.

The imprint control region on the H19/Igf2 cluster is unmethylated on the maternal allele.When the ICR is unmethylated it is bound by an insulator protein called CTCF.This protein insulates Igf2 from the downstream enhancers which would otherwise preferencially cause Igf2 expression .This renders the downstream enhansers free to act on H19  instead of Igf2 and enhance H19 expression.

Deletion of the maternal ICR or its hypermethylation results in activation of the normally silent maternal Igf2 allele and down-regulation of H19 .Paternal uniparental disomy at 11p15.5 loci also causes over expression of Igf2 and under expression of H19.

Down regulation of H19 which acts as a tumor suppressor and the upregulation of Igf2 which is a growth promoting gene leads to overgrowth and tumor development seen in wilms tumor.

DNA methylation alteration is an epigenetic change and epigenetic changes are passed on during cell division to daughter and granddaughter cells until they are actively erased. Once erased, though, they do not return.This could lead to enduring effects on the epigenome .

Periods of epigenetic reprogramming in cell are those during which if u alter the environment of the cells, or if u speed the reprogramming process up or  avoid them entirely there are adverse effects on the development of the organism due to poor efficiency of the reprogramming process

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine is a DNA methyl transferase inhibitor.It inhibit DNA methylation and functions by reactivating epigenetically silenced tumor suppressor genes .

Decitabine reverts hypermethylation induced gene silencing of tumor suppressor and other cancer-related genes .This hypermethylation plays a fundamental role in human tumorigenesis.The reversion of these epigenetic mutations  restores proliferation control and apoptosis sensitivity.

Metabolites of decitabine  become substrates for the DNA replication machinery and become incorporated into DNA. They are then recognized by the DNA methyltransferases as a natural substrate and the enzymes initiates the methylation reaction.A covalent bond is then formed between the incorporated metabolite of decitabine and the enzyme. This bond cannot be resolved as it normally would have been Thus the enzyme remains covalently bound to DNA and its DNA methyltransferase function is blocked. In addition, the covalent protein adduction also compromises the functionality of DNA and triggers DNA damage signaling, resulting in the degradation of trapped DNA methyltransferases As a consequence, methylation marks become lost during DNA replication.
