Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

There are two major alterations of the methylation levels that are found in the DNA of cancer cells. DNA methylation at CpG islands tend to be associated with gene silencing; in normal cells the CpG islands are usually not methylated, while in cancer cells they tend to be found hypermethylated. This disruption of methylation at CpG islands may be associated with the silencing of tumor suppressors genes; epigenetics changes like this can happen drastically and are easily perpetuated throughout the cells and through cells divisions, what associated with mutations in the genome, disruption of others epigenetic factors or activation of oncogenes may easily lead to tumors. The methylation in intergenic regions is believed to be responsible for maintenance of genomic integrity and stability. Almost with the same function, but acting on others kinds of structures and interactions between elements of the genome, the methylation of repetitive elements is also believed to be associated with maintenance of integrity and stability of the genome, but in this case it is because the silencing of repeats avoid mutations, transpositions, illegitimate recombinations and interferences from strong promoters. The disruption of this kinds of methylations happens with the hypomethylation of those elements, what tend to promote illegitimate recombinations and activations and transpositions of genes that are not supposed to occur in a health configuration of the genome, what may lead to cancer.

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine is a DNA methyltransferase inhibitor (DNMTi). It is incorporated into the DNA and prevents the DNMT from perpetuating the methylation along the cells divisions. When the DNMT comes to act, they bind irreversible at the DNMTi no longer promoting the methylation of that specific region of the DNA; since the DNMT bound and no longer act along the cells divisions, this makes the methylation to decrease along the replication of the DNA, what in tumor cells act inhibiting and preventing its growth.
