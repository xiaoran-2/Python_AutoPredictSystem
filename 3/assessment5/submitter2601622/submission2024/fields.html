  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to.            

Decitabine is a DNA-demethylating agent or a hypomethylating agent.

  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.             

It hypomethylates DNA by inhibiting DNA-methyltransferase. That means that Decitabine is a chemical analogue of the cytosine nucleoside used in DNA.

  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect.

The mechanisms used by this molecule are two: inhibition of DNA methyltransferase at low doses, causing hypomethylation of DNA, and direct cytotoxicity in abnormal hematopoietic cells in the bone marrow through its incorporation into DNA at high doses, resulting in cell death.
