Since Decitabine is a hypomethylating agent (a cytindine analog) it causes hypomethylation of DNA by inhibiting the enzyme DNA methyltransferase. Decitabine can only be incorporated into DNA strands, not RNA. Some cancers show signs of hypermethylation of genes that are supposed to be expressed. When areas of the genome are hypermetlyated, then those genes are silenced, and not expressed. If the p53 gene has been hypermethylated than the p53 gene will not be expressed. It is believed that Decitabine works by reversing some of the excess methylation at the CpG islands (and CpG island shores) and help return the cancer cells to their pre-cancer methylation state.

  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
The Igf2/H19 genes are located on the short arm of chromosine 11 and are part of a cluster of genes. In mammels, normally the insulin-like growth factor 2 (Igf2) gene is subjected to imprinting, so that the copy of the Igf2 gene from the mother is silenced but the H19 gene from the mother is expressed.  The copy of the Igf2 gene from the father is expressed, but not the H19 gene from the father. This silencing of the Igf2 gene from the mother happens through methylation of which causes this gene (from the mother) not to be express. If both copies of the IGF2 gene are active due to loss of imprinting, or from a genetic mutation, then both the Igf2 from the mother and the father are expressed. This causes too much insulin-like growth factor is produced. The H19 gene decreases growth while the Igf2 gene promotes cell growth. When too much IGF2 is expressed and the H19 gene is not expressed than cells grow too quickly and divide too often which may lead to cancer. Since the Igf2 gene promotes growth and then excess expression of Igf2 can lead to tumor growth.
