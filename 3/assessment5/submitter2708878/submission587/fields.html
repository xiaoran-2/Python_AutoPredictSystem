Methhylation of GpG islands in the promoter regions is normally associated to gene silencing in normal conditions. What is observed in cancer is that CpG islands in the promoter regions are hypermethylated and the CpG in the intergenic regions are hypomethylated (genome wide hypomethylation). this can contribute to genomic instability (hypomethylation of CpG intergenic regions) and much more gene silencing of Tumor suppressor genes (hypermethylation in CpG islands of Tumor suppressor promoter genes and this is considered as an alternative to mutations in tumor suppressor genes). The normal  function of DNA methylation is to prevent abnormal translocations (chromatine more packed with methylation). In cancer Intergenic regions are hypomethilated causing genomic instability.
