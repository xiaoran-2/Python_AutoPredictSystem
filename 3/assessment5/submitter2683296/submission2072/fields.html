Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

Gene expression in the cell is controlled via different mechanisms. While all cells have the same DNA in general, different tissues have different expression of this DNA. One factor that effect this expression is a promoter region called CpG islands. In general, genes are not expressed when the islands next to them are methylated. Methylation of CpG island prevents protein from transcribing DNA into mRNA.

In cancer, CpG islands tend to be hypermythelated. Unlike normal cells, hypermethlation decrease the levels of proteins necessary to control the cell.

For example, P53 is a famous protein that fixes DNA damage. When its CpG island is methylated, the expression is inhibited and DNA damage happens more frequently. Genes that fix cell damage, control the apoptosis(cell death)and manage growth may be suppressed by CpG methylation and it may lead to uncontrolled growth and cancer.

On the other hand, over 98% of human genome is heavily methylated to prevent viral genes and unwanted errors from happening. Repetitive elements in DNA is error porn regions due to the possibility of chromosome instability because of them. When those elements lost their methyl signals, the result may be changes in karyotype or mutation that lead to activation of oncogenes or having several copies of growth proteins.

Many intergenic regions are responsible for non-coding RNA which have regulatory rule is cell function and growth. Those regions are methylated in general. Similarly to repetitive elements, they may lose their methylation and be activated. This unleashes uncontrolled growth and lose of function in the cell, a characteristic feature of cancer.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

Insulin-like growth factor 2 (IGF-2) from paternal origin is expressed, while the same gene is suppressed in maternal chromosome. The main reason is a control region of CpG repeats and a noncoding RNA called H19.

In maternal allele, CpG island is not methylated and H19 is expressed. A protein called CTCF joins to CpG island and prevents IGF-2 promoters. The sum of these actions is suppression of maternal IGF-2.

On the other hand, paternal CpG island is methylated and IGF-2 is expressed freely, and H19 is not.

If maternal IGF-2 allele is not repressed due to CpG methylation, the outcome is overexpression of the growth factor. This causes more than 50% of Wilms tumors. Methylation of CpG island on both alleles (both parents) will prevent CTCF from binding to CpG repeats and prevent H19 expression.

Some cancers have hyper methylated regions such as the CpG islands. It leads to under expression of some genes (P53) and over expression of others (nclRNA). The enzyme involved in this procedure is called DNA methyltransferase (DNMT).

Decitabine is DNA-demethylating agent,which means that it causes hypomethylating of DNA. Its mechanism of action is by inhibiting DNA methyltransferase.

Regaining normal CpG methylation statues is essential in tumor suppression. Mainly because proteins that control cell growth and health can restore their funtion.
