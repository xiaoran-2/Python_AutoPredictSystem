Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

1.DNA methylation at  CpG islands occurs in the promoters of tumor supressor genes. So in the case of Normal cells methylation at CpG islands does not takes place.

2.In the cancer cells, CpG islands in promoter regions of tumor supressor genes are heavily methylated leading to inactivation of tumor supressor genes thus leading to cancer.

3. DNA methylation in intergenic and repetitive elements is established so as to pretect these regions from expression. Expression of these regions  and also the expressio of tumor supressor genes leads to cancer.

4. In Normal cells DNA methylation is established in intergenic regions  and repetitive elements leading to supression of these genes.

5.In case of cancer cells DNA methylation is these regions is removed so that these genes are free to express thus leading to cancer.

6.Disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic and repetitive elements leads to Reciprocal translocations, deletions and additions leading to progression of cancer.

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

1. In Paternal allele, DNA methylation is established in H19 gene, so CTCF cannot bind H19 allele. Therefore Igf2 gene is expressed.

2. In Maternal allele, DNA methylayion is not present in H19 gene, hence CTCF can bind and enhance the Enhancers to bind to H19 gene and hence H19 gene is expressed. Igf2 gene is not expressed in maternal allele.

3. In Wilm's tumor. the maternal allele reverses to paternal  allele, with Igf2 expression turned on and methylation pattern in H19 gene locus leading to repression of H19.

4. Disruption of  Imprinting at H19/Igf2 cluster leads to increased cell growth causing tumor.
