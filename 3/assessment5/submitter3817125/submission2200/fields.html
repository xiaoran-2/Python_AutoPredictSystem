Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation is one of the major epigenetic modifications which is involved in the regulation of gene expression. In the genome of an organism there is an specific pattern of methylation, which is involved  in the spatio-temporal expression of that particular gene and hence maintains homeostasis in the cell. Altered methylation pattern can be harmful to the cell and might lead to the cancer. In a normal cell genome is hypomethylated at CpG islands and hypermethylated in intergenic and repititive elements where as in cancer cell the situation reverses.

CpG islands are generally present in the promoter regions of the genes and since they are hypomethylated (that is,open chromatin), they promote the transcription of the genes to form proteins or non coding RNAs and perform normal function in the cell, where as intergenic and repititive elements are generally hypermethlated and which results into condensed chromatin and hence reduces the chances of recombination between non homologous chromosomes and hence increase genomic stability. But in a cancerous cell the situation reverses as said earlier. Hypomethylation of intergenic regions and repititive elements leads to open chromatin formation, more chances of recombination with different chromosomes and hence results in formation of faulty genomic complement.

Another important aspect in cancer formation is tumor suppressor and activator genes. There is a fine interbalance in the expression of these genes in the normal cells, whereas it has been seen in cancerous cells there is an altered expression of these genes. This observations can easily be explained by the changes in the overall genome methylation pattern of normal and cancerous cells as said above if tumor supressor gene is switched off and activator genes (c-myc) are on then it may lead to the cancer. Hence, DNA methylation plays an important role in cancer development.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

H19/Igf2 cluster is present on chromosome number 11. Inside the cluster there is an imprint control region (ICR) which can be either methylated and unmethylated. In this case, the maternal allele is unmethylated whereas the paternal allele is methylated hence it is paternally imprinted. Since, the maternal allele is unmethylated so it can be bound by CTCF (insulator protein) which insulates the Igf2 promoter from the downstream enhancer. So these enhancer are free to act on H19 and enhances its expression on maternal allele. While in case of paternal allele its opposite happens, since enhancers are free bind to Igf2 promoters they increase Igf2 production which is involved in cell growth.

In wilm's tumor, there is a methylation of the maternal allele as a result it starts to behave like a paternal chromosome. It causes the downstream enhancer to bind to the Igf2 promoter and hence the total amount of the Igf2 product becomes double. Since it causes the cell growth as a result the cells grow abnormally and lead to the tumor formation. It might occur due to the faulty formation of methylation marks during the epigenetic programming of the cell.
