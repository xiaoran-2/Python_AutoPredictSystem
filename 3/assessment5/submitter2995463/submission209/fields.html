Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

In normal cells, majority of CpG islands and genes promoters are non-methylated and genes are active. Cancer changes methylation profile of the cells, hypermethylating CpG islands. Such genes as Rb, MLH1 etc. become hypermethylated and their activity is blocked, provoking cells to uncontrolled cell division and metastasis. From the other side, level of hypermethylation (CIMP) is a good prognostic marker. For example, if MLH1 is hypermethylated in patients with colorectal cancer, it could mean good outcome, as this process is reversible and treatable.

Regarding intergenic and repetitive elements, they are silenced and hypermethylated in normal cells. One of the general features of the cancer cells that methylation their repetitive elements and intergenic regions are disrupted and hypomethylated. It causes genomic instability and as consequence illegitimate recombination of chromsome with disease marks accumulation.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

In normal cells, paternal allele of H19 is methylated while maternal allele is non-methylated. It gives expression of Igf2 from paternal gene only and thus regulated by imprinting. Alteration in methylation of H19/Igf2 cluster (hypermethylation of ICRs) results disactivation of H19 promotor, cahnges in enhancing of CTGF regulator protein and overexpression of Igf2 protein which causes uncontrolled growth promotion of kidney and Wilm's tumour in kids.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine is a deoxycytidine analogue and a hypomethylating agent. It is more stable and less toxic than azacitidine, classic DNA-demethylating agent. It belongs to the DNA methyltransferase inhibitors, lowering methylation status in cell through binding with DNMTs and thus activating necessary for cell homeostasis proteins. Among negative effects of its influence - that its impact isn't selective for cancer cells only; moreover, it affects all methylated normal genes as well.
