decitabine belongs to the class of drugs- DNA methyl transferase inhibitors which targets DNA methyl transferase. it is a nucleoside analogue which gets incorporated into the DNA on replication and binds irreversibly to DNA methyl transferase leading to their depletion inside the cell. it however depends on the cell proliferation for its activity, hence more effective in rapidly multiplying cancer cells

it causes drug induced reduction of DNA methylation or hypomethylation of the promoter regions(CpG islands), which contains the tumour supressor genes which may be hypermethylated in the cancer cells.they reverse gene methylation,allowing normal transcription to resume.

  • activation of CpG poor promoters(activation of oncogenes)

  • illegitimate combinations between the repeats.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

genomic imprinting is parent of origin specific monoallelic gene expression.

IGF2 and H19 are two imprinted genes located adjacent to each other. In  IGF2 gene, only the paternally inherited allele is expressed while the maternal allele is silent . The H19 gene is paternally silent while maternally expressed.

on the paternal allele, the ICR is imprinted, CTCF cant bind and hence there is no insulator action. DNA methylation spreads to H19 promoter to silence it,the enhancers then bind to Igf2 and activate it. only expressed in paternal allele.

on the maternal allele, ICR is unmethylated, hence binds to CTCF(insulator protein,insulates igf2 from enhancers). then secondary looping occurs and they act on H19 causing its activation.

it occurs when the maternal allele behaves like paternal allele

  • loss of imprinting-due to mutations, deletions
  • uniparental disomy-both copies being inherited from the father
  • epigenteic disruption causing loss of imprinting.
this is seen in beckwith wiedemann syndrome which shows a predisposition to wilm's tumour.

in wilm's tumour,there is loss of imprinting and hypermethlation of ICR on both paternal and maternal alleles.methylation of the H19 promoter, IGF2 turned on, and H19 turned off,  causing over expression of Igf2 which promotes cell growth tumorogenesis. it is often seen in pre-neoplastic tissues.
