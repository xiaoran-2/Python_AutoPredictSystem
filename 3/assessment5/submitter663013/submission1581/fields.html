Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

 

DNA methylation is usually connected with silencing of the gene expression. CpG islands - regions that contain a high frequency of CpG sites are found in coding region or in the promoters of genes. When in a promoter, the CpG islands are normally unmethylated. This is ensures the normal expression of the corresponding gene.

In cancer cells the CpG islands in promoters are hypermethylated and the corresponding genes – silenced. If these genes are involved in DNA regulation and cell growth and death the cell may start an unregulated growth.

 In cells of a tumor a genome wide hypomethylation and specific hypermethylation of is observed. This results in genome instability and a change in gene expression. The hypermethylation of  CpG islands in promoters of the genes makes their genes  silenced. High frequency of DNA methylation at CpG rich sites (termed CIMP) was identified in colorectal cancer and other like glioma or breast cancer.

 Intergenic regions belong to the noncoding DNA. Often they don't have known function but can interfere the nearby genes. Repetitive elements are parts of the DNA which are found in multiple copies in genome. Intergenic regions and repetitive elements are methylated in a normal cell which prevents these elements to be used for  transcription and to provoke instability.

 The genome-wide hypomethylation usual for cancer means that intergenic regions and repetitive elements are hypomethylated too. This may cause activation of promoters found there and as a result the expression of the nearby regions may be changed. The hypomethylation of IGR and repetitive elements is a symptom of a cancer in the tissue.

 Hypomethylation of repetitive elements is more significant as repetitive elements if they become active may make copies of themselves. The unmethylated repetitive elements may provoke recombination.  The following insertions and/or deletions disrupt the integrity of DNA and cause genomic instability. This instability is a cause of the appearance and the progression of cancer.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

Igf2 and H19 genes on chromosome 7 are reciprocally imprinted. Igf2 is activated on the paternal chromosome and H19 is activated on the maternal chromosome. This is done by methylation of Igf2 on the maternal allele and unmethylation on the paternal allele and methylation of H19 on the paternal allele, and unmethylation on the maternal allele. This way Igf2 is expressed on the paternal chromosome while H19 on the maternal.

 In Wilm’s tumour cells the normal imprinting of Igf2 and H19 is disturbed and the maternal chromosome uses a paternal type of expression with Igf2 activated on, and H19 deactivated. H19 is known as a tumor suppressor gene and it's deactivation causes increased cell growth.

Decitabine is a new cancer medicine with a commercial name Dacogen. Decitabine is an demethylation or hypomethylating agent which inhibiting DNA methyltransferase and this way decreases methylation.

The anti-tumour effect is caused by decreasing methylation of the tumor suppressing genes and restoring their function at one hand and by its antimetabolitic effect which causes death of the rapidly dividing cancer cells on the other hand.
