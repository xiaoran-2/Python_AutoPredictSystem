Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation at CpG islands will supress the involved gene. In cancer many of these normaly unmethylated islands will be hypermethylated. The hypermethylated CpG islands turn OFF tumor supressing genes, thus enabling unregulated growth and possibly cancer.

Normally intergenic and repetitive areas are hypermethylated. In cancer they become hypomethylated, allowing them to translocate more easily thus causing genomic instability. They may also effect transcription and turn on oncogenes. 

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

Igf2 is a maternally imprinted gene, ie, it is methylated and turned off on the maternal allele and unmethylated and transcribed by the paternal allele. In wilms tumor Igf2 is expressed by both alleles and thus Igf2 is over expressed.

Igf2, insulin like growth factor2 gene is involved in growth, and thus if overproduced is implicated in embryonic solid organ tumors like wilms tumor. Also H19 is linked to Igf2 production such that when Igf2 transcription increases H19 transcription is decreased. Since H19 may be a tumor supressor, down regulation may contribute to oncogenesis.

Disruption of the Igf2 /H19 locus lalso lead to 'tissue growth' diseases like Beckworth Wiedeman syndrome, characterised by overgrowth.
