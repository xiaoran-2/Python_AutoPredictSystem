Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine belongs to the class of epigenetic inhibitors of DNMTi(DNA methyl transferases inhibitor).It is used for myelodysplastic syndrome progressing to AML.Since DNMTis are nucleoside analogues so that when replication occurs they are incorporated into DNA and when DNMTs come and bind to it to copy the methylation to the daughter strand then that DNMT is bound irreversibly and can no longer be released. This shows that they are replication dependent and hence will affect cancer cells to the maximum.Thus they lead to demethylation.
