Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

First of all, we have to say that CpG islands are usually unmethylated. Due to methylathion is an inactive epigenetic mark, CpG islands tend to be protected from methylation. When methylation occurs, meCpG in a CpG island is associated with the formation of a repression chromatin structure, then meCpG can prohibit transcriptional factor binding and alter gene expression. In cancer, what we can see is an hypermethylation of CpG island and this cause a silencing of the tumour suppressor gene which can lead, with other features, to cancer. This hypermethylation tend to differ by the different types of tumours. We can also see an hypermethylation in ICRs and it can lead to an overexpression of growth promoting genes.

Either intergenic regions or repetitive regions are usually methylated. The function of the methylated intergenic regions is to maintain genomic stability working with Dnmt1. Then it can maintain a normal karyotype.

The function of DNA methylation at repetitive elements is to maintain genomic integrity; it is done by silencing of repeats to prevent transposition, by mutation of the repeats to prevent transposition, by silencing of repeats, to avoid transcriptional interference from strong promoters and by methylation of repeats to prevent illegitimate recombination; another function of this kind of methylation is a genome defense model, for example to protect the genome from transposable elements. These regions can be disrupted, producing cancer. These disruptions consist on the hypomethylation of those parts, and then these parts can’t maintain neither the stability nor integrity. It can success an illegitimating recombination between repeats, the activation of repeats and transposition and the activation of cryptic promoters and disruption to neighbouring genes.

As in the hypermethylation, hypomethylation effect can be stage specific and different tumours can have different dependencies.
