Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

The paternal allele the ICR and H19 promoters are  methylated and thus silenced and enhancers can access Igf2 to promote expression. 

In the maternal allele neither are methylated and CTCF binds to the ICR thus blocking enhancers access to the Igf2 and silencing expression. H19 is active

The maternal ICR is hypermethylated and the H19 promoter locus as well (identical to the paternal). This results in both Igf2 sites being active and  results in an overexpression of Igf2 leading to loss of growth regulation.

Disruption can either result in expression of both genes or neither and thus lead to over or under regulation of controlled functions
