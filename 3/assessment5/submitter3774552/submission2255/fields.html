Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation at the CpG island is responsible for silencing of gene expression mechanism. Hypermethylationat the CpG island will lead to silencing of the tumor suppressor genes in cancer.

DNA methylation at the intergenic  region and repetitive elements is responsible for the maintaining genomic integrity. Hypomethylation of the intergenic region and the repetitive elements happens in cancer. This will lead toillegimate recombination between repeats, activation of repeats and transposition, activation of cryptic promotorsand disruption of the neighboring gene.

Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

In the paternal chromosome the imprint control region is methylated so the CTCF cannot bind to the imprint control region so there will be no insulation of the Igf2 will occur and the enhancer will be free to act on the Igf2 and promote the Igf2 expression. In the maternal chromosome the imprint control region is unmethylated so the CTCF will bind to the imprint control region which lead to insulation of the Igf2 from downstream to the enhancers. This will lead to the silencing of the Igf2 on the maternal chromosome.

In Wilm’s tumor the Cdkn1 is hypermethylated which will lead to over expression of the Igf2. The Cdkn1 is a tumor suppressor since it restrict growth of cells while the Igf2 is an oncogene since it promotes growth.

The Decitabine is an epigenetic drug and specifically DNAmethylating agent . It reduces methylation by acting on an enzyme called EZH2, this enzyme attaches methyl groups to the histone protein. The histone over methylation declines so the silenced neighboring genes so called tumor suppressor genes will no longer be silenced.
