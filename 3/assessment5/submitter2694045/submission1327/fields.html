Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands is to halt the transcription of the genes it precedes. In cancer the CpG islands are hyper methylated, especially those followed by tumor suppressors thereby the cell does not suppress the growth and cell division, which is not the same case for normal cells. We can also witness hypo methylation on those CpG islands in cancer cells promoting Oncogenes, genes that can make cells divide uncontrollably. Normally intragenic regions and repetitive elements are methylated in a normal cell because of their instability if found as Euchromatin. Repetitive elements if not methylated can cause recombination, insertions and/or deletions that may directly harm genes responsible for cell division and cell growth, and this is what happens in most of the cases in Cancer : Repetitive elements become Hypo methylated thereby causing deletions, insertions, or recombination. So to summarize, in Cancer CpG islands tend to be hypermethylated, while intragenic and repetitive elements are hypomethylated. This knowledge can contribute greatly to our treatment of Cancer, since for some kinds of Cancers we can target Methylation to hinder the progress of Cancer.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

DNMT is responsible for keeping DNA methylations through the replicated DNA strand. Decitabine is a DNMT inhibitor. Decitabine inhibits the DNA methylation from occurring in the DNA strand replicated because of its inhibition of DNMT. Since most of cancer cells have hyper methylated CpG islands preceding Tumor suppressors and causes the cell to divide uncontrollably, Decitabine decreases this methylation when it inhibits DNMT, therefore causing Tumor suppressors genes to be active again and take control of the cell division.

Methylation of the parental allele imprint control region causes Igf2 to be expressed rather than H19. Igf2 promotes growth. In the maternal imprinting control region there is no methylations thereby Igf2 is not activated, and the enhancers act to promote H19. This is what happens in a normal cell. Only one Igf2 is functional. In a diseased cell we can witness either both of the alleles methylated or both of them not methylated.
