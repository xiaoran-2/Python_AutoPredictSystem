Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Among the epigenetic-based drugs currently marketed, Decitabine is used to treat myelodysplastic syndromes, which result in myeloid leukaemia (AML). This drug has a mechanism of DNA hypomethylation that inhibits neoplastic cell growth.

For this reason, Decitabine is used as anti-tumor treatment: when it is incorporated in to the DNA (upon replication), it interferes irreversibly in the activity of DNA methyltransferases (DNMTs). This action can inhibit, by altering the epigenome, development of tumor cells. Strictly, Decitabine is considerated a DNA-methyltransferases inhibitor (DNMTi).

As mentioned previously (Q1), methylation of CpG island is increased in a cancer cell, because of CGI hypermethylation. If you do not add a methyl group to the promoters of tumor suppressor genes, they can continue to play its role to prevent uncontrolled cell proliferation.
