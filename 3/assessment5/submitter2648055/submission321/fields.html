Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

 

The normal function of DNA methylation at CpG island ( usually in promoter region) is silencing of genes. CpG island are usually unmethylate so genes are active . Sometimes there is a disruption in the methylation pattern this can be a result of : 1. Mutation in chromatin related enzymes such as DMMT's or chromatin remodelers which can cause global changes in epigenetic. 2. Faulty targeting or altered composition of epigenetic complexes 3.Changed in the spatio- temporal organization of the nuclear architecture or loss of boundaries might cause altered nuclear territories , disrupt ordered epigenetic patterns . These process can lead  to methylation of    CpG islands and this causes silencing of genes some of  them are tumor suppressors  and that and cause unregulated growth – cancer.

In intergenic regions and repetitive elements the CpGs are usually methylated . This is important for silencing repeats and preventing transposition of repeat elements and prevents illegitimate recombinations. These are all very important in order to keep genome stability. In cancer because of methylation disruption ( as explained above) which can be a result from altered external signaling due to permanent stress, intergenic region and repeats are hypomethylated and that causes genome instability activate cryptic splice site and causes cancer.
