Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation is a biochemical process involving the covalent addition of a methyl group at the 5' carbon of the cytosine ring, primarily at cytosine–guanosine dinucleotides (CpGs).

In cancer the  mechanisms that interfere with   the normal DNA methylation marks are  genomic hypomethylation or  hypermethylation of  CpG islands that is  associated with silencing the expression of  genes, sometimes growth supressor genes  .

DNA methylation is  involved in silencing of repetitive element ,  transposition, and   maintaining the genome integrity  - methylation of the  intergenic regions.

Cancer alters the normal layout of epigentic marks  :  the intergenic regions and repetitive elements are hypomethilated causing instability  of
the genome  by  promoting  illegitimal deletions, translocations and insertions .

Patternal allele has this imprinted  region methylated   , hence CTCF can no longer bind and  promote Igf2's expression , therefore, Igf2 is expressed only from the paternal allele and not the maternal allele.

H19 is active only on the materna allele as on the paternal allele the   DNA methylation is spreading downstream into the H19 promoter , sillencing it.

The  loss of imprinting in this case reflects that  there is an appearance of two paternal like alleles and loss of the maternal like allele,producing too much Igf2.

Individuals having this mutation are predisposed to childhood tumours such as Wilms tumor which is found in the kidney  by overexpression of growth promoting genes or loss of the  suppressor  genes .
