DNA methylation alters gene expression. DNA methylation at CpG islands will cause the silencing or halt the transcribtion of the genes around the region. In cancer, there is there is aberrent methylation in the form of hypermethylation of the CpG islands.

  DNA hypermehtylation at the CpG islands can cause or contribute to cancer by transcribtional silencing of the genes around the CpG island that may contain sequences for tumour supressor genes that are suppose to halt the growth and multiplication of cancerous cells.

  Repetitive elements are long repeats of a single nucleotode, together with intergenic regions are supressed in normal cells by DNA methylation to prevent them from incerting into other regions of the genome and cause genomic instability.
