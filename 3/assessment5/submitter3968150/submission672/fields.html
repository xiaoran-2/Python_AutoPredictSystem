    The reason why DNA methylation has effects that last beyond the period of drug treatment is that it is a mitotic heritable process which can be maintained by enzymes like Dnmt 1. After cell division, the two daughter cells have some hemimethylated DNA. The Dnmt 1 can recognize these hemimethylated DNA substrates and then methylate the unmethylated strand of the hemimethylated DNAs. Therefore some drugs’ DNA methylation effects can last until the methylation is activated removed.

    Since these drugs’ fundamental mechanism for treatment disease is DNA methylation, the DNA methylation reprogramming stage may be the sensitive period of these drugs. The sensitive period is a period when the drugs’ effects on DNA methylation may influence the next generation development. It includes the period from primordial germ cell development to mature gamete and the period of pre-implantation. If a pregnant woman take these drugs which can alter the DNA methylation process in her body, it can also influence the fetus’ DNA methylation reprogramming process during the pre-implantation process.
