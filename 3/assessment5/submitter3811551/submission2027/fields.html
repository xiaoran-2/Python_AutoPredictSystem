Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

There are some cases when dusruption of imprinting patterns can lead to  Wilm's tumour. Let us consider as an example H19/lgf2 cluster. For normal cells in paternal allele ICR region is methylated and therefore CTCF is not bound to it enabling enhancers to bind to lgf2, leading to its expression. In contrast, maternal allele is unmethylated therefore enhancers can act over H19 expresing this gene, however lgf2 is not expressed. In Wilm's tumour both maternal and paternal ICR's are methylated, therefore there is a double dose expression of lgf2. Because lgf2 is growth promoting gene the increased dose of it will result in Wilm's tumour.

Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

         Both hypermethylation by locus specific manner and hypomethylation by genome wide manner can lead to cancer development. In normal cells CpG islands in the promotor region of many tumour suppressor genes  are hypomethylated, therefore it is active  and tumour formation is depressed. However in cancer cells these islands tend to be hypermethylated. This hypermethylation can influence single gene or sets of gene as well.Usually it is found that hypermethylation occurs at CpG island shores, in other words the region 2kb neighboring to the CpG island can also be methylated. This process(methylation of CpG islands) is continues and reversible , so as time does by some epimutation might accumulate leading to cancer development.  

        The normal cells have methylated intergenic regions and repetitive elements, thus maintaining the genomic stability.This is partly accomplished  by the heterochromatin structure formation, because methylated chrosome tends to be compacted and inactive. However, when these regions are hypomethylated illegitimate recombinations between homologous repeats, unwanted activation of some promoters expressing unwanted proteins, activation of transposonal activity, deletions and insertions can occur. All of the abovementioned processes are abnormal therefore they might change normal functions of the cells and usually leads to some type of the cancer.

Decitabine is DNA-demethylating agent which might be used to treat the myelodysplastic syndrome. This agent can erase the methyl groups thus weakening hypermethylation status, therefore some tumour suppressing genes can be reactivated after several divisions.
