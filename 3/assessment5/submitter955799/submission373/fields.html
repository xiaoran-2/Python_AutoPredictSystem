Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.


Written Exam Answers Q1.pdf

Q1.
1. Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
Gene methylation has the ability to silence genes and promoter regions.


2. Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
Methylation of CpG islands in cancer often are hypermethylated and thus suppress genes
which regulate normal proliferation.


3. Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to disease.
DNA Methylation at CpG islands often suppresses gene expression of regulatory genes
allowing excess proliferation.


4. Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive
elements.
Methylation silences these regions


5. Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted
in cancer.
Hypomethylation of these regions may occur on a large scale.


6. Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements
contributes to disease.
Hypomethylation of the intergenic regions may lead to loss of imprinting, and to the
generation of lincRNAs which in turn can suppress such genes as p53. Also if global
hypomethylation occurs then many of the other pathway elements are interfered with and
normal proliferation is disrupted.

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour.

Written Exam Answers Q2.pdf

Q2.
1. Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2
expression status.
The paternal allele is hypermethylated and thus Igf2 is expressed.


2. Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2
expression status.
The maternal allele is not hypermethylated and thus H19 is expressed. Igf2 is not expressed.


3. Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour.
Hypermethylation of the maternal ICR results in excess expression of the Igf2 growth factor.


4. Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to disease.
Loss of imprinting can result in hypermethylation and excess expression of Igf2. This results
in excess and uncontrolled proliferation.

Q3.
1. Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to.
Nucleoside analog


2. Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
Blocks and reverses hypermethylation allowing for normal growth and proliferation of stem
cells so as to reduce blast load in patient. May also in certain patients result in loss of
hypermethylation imprinting.


3. Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect.
In MDS for example it acts as a hypomethylation factor and as with azacytadine may also
reduce imprinting of dysplastic stem cells.

Imprinting may be changed and then can propagate through mitotic proliferation.

Sensitive period is when the cell is going through mitosis and imprinting may be lost.
