Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

In a normal cell, CpG islands are hypomethylated  and DNA methylation cannot be found in CpG islands. But when the normal cell becomes a cancer one, more CpG island are methylated than usual. This phenomenon is called CGI hypermethylation.

 

DNA methylation at CpG islands is asociate with tumour suppressors(a gene that protects a cell from one step on the path to cancer).The mechanism of happening of deseases is a cancer cell can make tumor suppressor genes inactivated. Cancer cell could do that genetically or epigenetically by silencing the tumor suppressor gene by locking in acyl in active state. And since DNA methylation is mitotically heritable, this means that this is a very good locking down mechanism in terms of epigenetic silencing. According to Knudson hypothesis, tumor regenesis may happen when both copies of the tumor suppressor or both copies of the tumor suppressor and infections of other hits(e. g. activation of uncle gene) silenced. CpG island methylation progresses with time. When time passing by, as the cancer progresses, methylation level of CpG islands increases and makes tumour suppressors silenced,as a result, cancer happens.

 

Unlike CpG islands, intergenic regions and repetitive elements in genomes are methylated in normal cell while being hypomethylated in cancer cells.

Hypomethylation of intergenic regions and repetitive elements cause genomic instability for the reasons such as loss of imprinting and reactivation of transponsable elements. It brings about deletions, reciprocal traslocation, insertions, duplications to make karyotypes abnormal. Finally, mutations of the karyotypes turn normal cells into cancer ones.
