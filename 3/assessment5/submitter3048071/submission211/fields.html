  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The fuction of DNA methylation at CpG islands is to suppress function of   gene ,situated downstream to methylated CpG islands, by avoiding it being transcribed.Having methylation at CpG islands makes the nucleosome densely packed. Transcriptional factors can not access and can not bind to densely packed nucleosomes.Also transcriptional factors do not bind to genes with methylated promoters.Therefore genes having methylated CpG islands are not expressed. In normal celles CpG island are methylated , where downstream genes should be suppressed or silenced.Proteins encoded by these genes are not important for function of the cell.CpG island and its shores of tumour suppressor genes are not methylated in normal cells.  But we consider cancer cell , locus specific hyper methylation can be seen at CpG islands  and CpG island shores of tumour suppressor genes.Therefore tumour suppressor genes are silenced.  As tomour supressor genes are not expreesed, cell undergoes uncontrolled replications.So the growth of the cell is not restricted. Normal tissue becomes first hyperplasia , secondly neoplasia and ends up with invasion state.In normal cells intergenic regions and repititive elements are methylated. Therefore these regions are densely packed with histone proteins and this pattern of methylation protects chromosomes getting translocated , deleted or inserted.Silencing of repititive elements maintains genomic integrity (stability) and avoids transcriptional interference from strong promoters.Methylation of repeats may prevent illegitimate recombinations(combinations between non -homologous chromosomes) . In cancer cells genome -wide hypomethylation can be seen. Intergenic and repititive elements are hypomethylated.This leads to genomic instability .These hypomethylated chromosomes are highly likely to be tanslocated ,deleted and inserted.Activation of repeats can disrupt neighbouring genes.Illegitimate recombinations occur.
