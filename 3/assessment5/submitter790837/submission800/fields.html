DNA methylation at CpG islands in normal cells are hypomethylated. So there is no methylation at CpG islands. On the other hand in cancer cells the CpG islands are hypermethylated. Most likely CpG islands are found in the promoters of tumor supresssor. By hypermethylation tumor supressors are not able to be expressed (they are silenced) so tumor can come to existance. On the other hand the intragenic regions and repetative elments show a different methylation pattern in normal and in cancer cells.
