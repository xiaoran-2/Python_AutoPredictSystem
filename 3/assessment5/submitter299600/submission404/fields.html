DNA methylation (DM) is one of the most amazing processes of the cell regulation. It occurs at CpG islands, which is a Methyl group will add to the 5'-C of cytosine. DM regulates gene expression (gene repression by inhibiting the binding of transcription factors to access gene), X chromosome inactivation, chromatin structure, the formation of heterochromatin and sometimes are inheritable.
 
DM pattern is unique from tissue to tissue, time by time. Global hypomethylation is a sight of cancer. Nevertheless, promoter hypermethylation also indicate that cancer and many diseases development can happen. Ex: DNA methylation is a biomarker for cardiovascular disease risk.
 
DNA methylation in intergenic regions (IR) & repetitive elements (RE) are maintain genomic integrity and RE are also a genome defense model.
 
In cancer, IR & RE are hypomethylation, let to the whole genome instability. In other hand, silencing or mutaion occur in RE will prevent transposition and prevent transcription. It all takes part in the cancer and disease development.

Some cancer, such as AML, some hypermethylated genes are correlated with the gene "auto-repair". They are commonly tumour suppressor genes (APC, RASSF1A, FHIT, p16 INK4A,...). So, when decitabine de-methylate these genes, it let to a tumour destruction.
