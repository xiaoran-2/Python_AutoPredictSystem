Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine its a DNA-demethylating agent or hypomethylating agent. It hypomethylates DNA by inhibiting DNA methytransferase, in other words, is a drug that inhibits DNA methylation. "DNA methylation is a biochemical process involving the addition of a methyl group to the cytosine or adenine DNA nucleotides".

DNA methylation is also an important regulator of gene transcription and a large body of evidence has demonstrated that genes with high levels of 5-methylcytosine in their promoter region are transcriptionally silent. 

Aberrant DNA methylation patterns - hypermethylation and hypomethylation compared to normal tissue-have been associated with a large number of human malignancies. Hypermethylation typically occurs at CpG islands in the promoter region and is associated with gene inactivation. Decitabine may have an anti-tumour effect because if " acts by inhibiting DNA methytransferase" genes like the tumour supressor gene are like to be more active(less methylated) and then effective against tumour supression.
