Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

The maternal chromosome 11 expressess H19 gene & the paternal, Igf2 gene.

The Imprint Control Region(ICR) on maternal allele is unmethylated & binds CTCF,generating an insulator.This insulator prevents Igf2 from accesing the Enhancers shared with H19.Thus, the Enhancers act predominantly on H19 gene & is expressed.The ICR on paternal allele is methylated & doesn't bind CTCF so an Insulator Blocker is not generated which blocks Igf2.In additional to this the methylated ICR also methylates H19 gene & silences it.The Enhancers act predominantly on Igf2, leading to it's expression.

When the maternal ICR is also methylated,leading to loss of imprint.The condition is similar to that of uniparental paternal disomy.Either of the parental alleles express Igf2 & neither of them express H19.H19 silencing & Igf2 overexpression are closely related ,the former results in Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome & the latter in Wilm's tumor.H19 codes for a long noncoding RNA which acts as riboregulator,silencing it causes childhood cancers.whenever H19 is silenced it results in re-expression of Igf2 allele,which encodes for a embryonal growth factor -Igf2 protein,the cause of neoplasm.H19/Igf2 cluster is present in WT2(Wilm's Tumor) region.WT1-a tumor suppressor gene.It's mutation also results in Wilm's tumor accompanying WAGR syndrome or Deny-Drash syndrome.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine is a FDA approved DNMT inhibitor.Decitabine is a nucleoside analogue, so gets incorporated in the DNA.When DNA methyltransferase enzyme encounters it on it's way of transcribing the DNA methylation mark from parent strand to daughter strand,Decitabine irreversibly binds DNMT & stops the abnormal pattern of DNA methylation.The normal tumor suppressor genes are erased of hypermethylation marks & are switched back on, to bring cancer cells under control.Yet ,their mechanism of action is still obscure.

Decitabine is DNMT dependent.DNMTs are transcription dependent.Therefore Decitabine is mainly transcription dependent.The cancer cells have high transcription rate so Decitabine has anti-tumour effect particularly at low doses,at high doses they are non-specific inhibitors(affects the normal cells also).Decitabine is used in Myelodysplastic syndrome which has progressed to AML.According to recent research works they are also useful in some solid tumors.
