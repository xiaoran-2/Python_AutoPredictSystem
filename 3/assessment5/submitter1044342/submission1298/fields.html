Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands is regulation of gene expression of genes which have a promotor including the CpG islands. In cancer cells they are often hypermethylated and thereby cause silecing of certain tumour supressor genes which, in response, promotes tumorigenesis. Nevertheless, the normal function of found DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements causes them to stay transcriptionaly not active. This type of methylation is commonly lost in cancer due to the global hypomethylated phenotype of cancer cells. This causes genomic instability, for example chromosomal translocations, chromosomal inversion or deletions often encountered in cancer cells, which causes a mutator phenotip and a faster progression of a disease.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 

Decitabine is an epigenetic inhibitor which belongs to a class of DNA-demethylating agents, which means that it causes a removal of DNA methylation in rapidly dividing cancer cells. By eliminating of the hypermethylation of promotor CpG islands of certain tumour suppresor genes which is a common ocurance in cancer cells it can restore their normal function and thereby act as an anti-tumour agent.
