Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

In cancer, DNA methylation is altered in several ways. First, at CpG islands, normally is free from methylation because they are very dense, is hypermethylated by DNA methylation in cancer cells, and the underlying genes of the CpG islands are typically silenced. This leads to cancer because CpG islands are promoters for tumor suppressor genes, therefore the genes silenced by hypermethylation are these tumor suppression that are supposed to stop cancer proliferation, and with these genes silenced, the tumor cell is free to reproduce asexually in the body. Normally, intergenic regions and repetitive elements in DNA are used for chromatin regulation in gene regulation, which is maintained by DNA methylaton. However in cancer, DNA methylation is disrupted and intergenic regions and repetitive elements do not properly regulate chromatin, which means that any cells reproduced from the tumor cells cannot be regulated back into normal cells, which contributes to cancer by making its proliferation immortal and absolute.
