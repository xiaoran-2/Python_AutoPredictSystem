Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

In normal cells CpG islands are regions between 300-3000 bp in length, these are found approximately in 60% of the promoters in mammal genes. For these reason and in order to maintain adequate levels of gene expression, they are maintained free of methylation. However in cancer cells, CpG islands appear hypermethylated, this change in the methylation status, in the promoters of cancer cells, causes silencing of tumor suppressor genes, contributing   in the maintenance and growing of cancer cells. On the other hand, in normal cells intergenic regions and repetitive elements are kept methylated, while in cancer cells these parts of the genome appear hypotmethylated, contributing with recombination and genomic instability in cancer cells.

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

 Imprinted genes are expressed in a monoallelic way normal cells, most of these genes are involved in growth or suppression . For example the imprinting control region (ICR) in the parental allele from the cluster H19/Igf2 is methylated, for this reason the enhancers can interact with  Igf2, producing the expression of Igf2 from  the paternal allele. on the other hand, ICR on the maternal allele in cluster H19/Igf2 is unmethylated for this reason the enhancers will act on H19 cluster preventing the expression of Igf2. However in Wilm's tumor, ICRs in  maternal and paternal alleles are methylated, producing double douse of Igf2, promoting excessive  the cell growth, as a consequence the tumor that is observed in these disease.
