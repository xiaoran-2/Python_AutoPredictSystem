Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The degree of DNA methylation in CpG islands in promoter regions determines the expression of the subsequent genes.
Normally, CpG island are protected from methylation. Methylation means silencing of gene expression.
There are 2 ways how me-CpG leads to silencing of gene expression:
1) it can form repressive chromatins structures
2) it can avoid binding of transcription factors.
In normal cells CpG islands usually are hypomethylated wheras hypermethylation of the CpG islands is associated with cancer. In this case a tumor-suppressor gene is silenced via the methylated promoter region.

Intergenic regions and Repetitive elements normally are methylated, this maintains genomic stability. Methylation helps preventing transposition and illegitimate recombination of DNA fragments. In cancer cells deletions, insertions and reciprocal translocations are observed, because unmethylated Intergenic regions and Repetitive elements are unprotected.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
Decitabine (Dacogen) is an anti-cancer drug used in chemotherapy and can be classified in 2 categories:
1) DNA-demethylation agent
Gene expression in cells is regulated by DNA methylation.The activity of suppressor genes influences cell division and growth. When suppressor genes are blocked, cell division processes get out of control which promotes cancer. Increased DNA methylation leads to blockage of demethylation/interferences of DNA methylation. By this demethylation process, the normal function of the tumor suppressor genes is regained and control over cell growth is restored.
2) Antimetabolite
Decitabine is a synthetical nucleosid and acts as analogon of cytidin. As Decitabine is very similar to cytidin, it is metabolised in the cell replication process and shows its cytotoxic effect in rapidly duplicating cancer. Cell division is disturbed which leads to death of the cancer cell.
