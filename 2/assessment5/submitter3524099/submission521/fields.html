  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
The epigenetic processes,  like DNA -methylation, are susceptible to chemical intervention because they are reversible. The overactive of the EZH2, an enzyme that attaches methyl groups to histone proteins, produces uncontrolled cell growth and cause cancer.
Decitabine is a DNA-demethylating agent. It is used to treat myelodysplastic syndromes, the precursors of acute myelogenous leukaemia. Decitabine inhibit the activity of an enzyme called EZH2. With this treatment the amount of histone overmethylation declined dramatically, it reduces the proliferation of tumour cells.
