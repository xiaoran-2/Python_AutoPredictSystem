Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The normal function of the methylation of CpG sites in the promoters of genes is to inhibit the expression of a gene. In the case of cancer, the CpG islands tend to be methylated, being the intergenic regions and repetitive elements less methylated.Methylation of repetitive elements and intergenic regions aims to stabilize the genome, limiting the mobility of these components and reducing the rate of mutation associated with it (deletions, insertions, translocations, activation / gene silencing).
Howewer , Intergenic regions and repetitive elements in a cancer cell are hypomethylated, helping the mobility of these elements and genome instability. This hypomethylation of intergenic regions and repetitive elements cause deletions, insertions and reciprocal translocations, generating activation/silencing of genes and greater genetic instability.

Decitabine is a class of epigenetic inhibitor that belongs to DNA-demethylating agents. Decitabine demethylates certain regions of the genome and it is useful to treat myelodysplastic syndromes, acute leukemia of the myelogenous precursors.
