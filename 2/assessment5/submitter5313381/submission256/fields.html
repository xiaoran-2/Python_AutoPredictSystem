Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

CpG islands are hypomethylated in normal cells. Hypermethylation of CpG islands silences tumor suppressor genes. Then, the gen is unable to stop uncontrolled cell growth, causing cancer. However, intergenic regions and repetitive elements are hypermethylated in normal cells. Genome-wide hypomethylation (mainly related to intergenic regions and repetitive elements) causes genome instability that could involve deletions, reciprocal translocations or insertions, giving an abnormal karyotype. These alterations could enhance tumorigenesis.
Therefore, DNA methylation could enhance or suppress tumorigeneis, depending on the tissue involved in the cancer: depletion of DNA methylation should result in tumorigenesis suppression in cancer generated by tumour suppressor hypermethylation, whereas it should enhance tumorigenesis in cancer caused by chromosomal instability.  
Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

Cluster H19/Igf2 is an example of how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer. ICR in paternal allele is methylated and DNA methylation at ICR blocks CTCF insulator protein binding at ICR and it enables downstream enhancers to act on Igf2 and it can be expressed. On the other hand, ICR is unmethylated on maternal allele and CTCF insulator element binds ICR. Therefore, enhancers are free to act on H19 long non coding RNA and Igf2 gene is silent because CTCF is insulating Igf2 from enhancers. However in Wilm´s tumour, ICR is also methylated in maternal allele and enhancers can act on Igf2 and it can be expressed. So, double dose of Igf2 can be expressed in parental alleles (normal paternal + abnormal maternal). As Igf2 is a growth promoting factor, it helps cell uncontrolled replication in kidney Wilm´s tumour.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
Decitabine is a DNA demethylating agent used to treat myelodysplastic síndromes that are the precursors of acute myelogenous leukaemia. This drug causes DNA hypomethylation in hypermethylated tumor-suppressed genes and leads to epigenetic reactivation of these genes. Then, it results in tumorigenesis suppression.
