  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.

In the normal cell, the CpG islands present in the promoters of the tumour suppressor genes are hypo-methylated which renders it active in controlling growth and hence tumour. whereas In the cancer cells , the CpG islands are hyper-methylated and the percentage of these increase with increase in the age, which shows the increasing risk of cancer at older ages as these are usually present in promoters of genes especially tumour suppressor genes. The level of the hyper-methylation of the CpG island increases with the  progression of the cancer. This DNA hyper-methylation can lead to the activation of not only the silencing of the tumour suppressor genes but also the inactivation of  various cellular pathways like the DNA repair, cell cycle and apoptosis, cell adherence and detoxification etc. all of which play an important role in the maintenance of the normal state of the cell.

In the normal cells , the intergenic regions and the repititive elements are highly methylated which may interfere with the transcription and translation process  or may not allow the expression cryptic promoters which may lead to the production of the RNA's or certain protein that stimulate tumours. whereas in the cancer cells, it is the vice versa wherein in there is hypo-methylation of the elements, which may activate the cryptic promoters which may lead to abberations or they may be insertions or deletions due to genomic instability as adequate methylation is necessary to prevent degradation the chromosome.
Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
H19/Igf2 cluster a set of alleles that are always expressed reciprocally and they share enhancers present downstream. Ins normal case, in the paternal allele the H19 gene and the Imprint control gene  ( ICR )  upstream to the H19 gene are hypermethylated which silences the H19 gene and allows the Igf2 gene to be stimulated by the enhancers thereby allowing expression of growth, while on the maternal allele a insulating protein called CTCF binds to the ICR and  prevents the expression of the Igf2 gene so that H19 gene is expressed and its expression is enhanced by the enhancers.

In the case of will's tumor , there is hypermethylation on the ICR and the downstream H19 gene which results in the enhancers inducing and enhancing the expression of the Igf2 gene ie there is biallelic expression which results in uncontrolled growth ultimately leading to tumor.
