Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The normal function of DNA methylation of CpG islands is the formation of repressive chromatin structures resulting in gene silencing by condensing chromatin.Methylated islands are bound by CpG binding proteins MeCP1 and 2 which have their own transcriptional repressive domain.
MeCP1 and 2 also recruit other protein partners that help in condensing the chromatin at these loci.

DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted by methylation at normally unmethylated  CpG island loci . Usually 
silencing  promotors of tumor suppressor genes. 

Tumor suppressor genes silenced no longer slow cell division,repair DNA mistakes,and mediate programmed apoptosis which often occurs as the result of DNA errors or mutations .The resultant cell lines are selected for over normal cells .They have higher ,uncontrolled  replication rates, propagate DNA mutations and mistakes and don't undergo apoptosis.

Normal DNA methylation at intergenic sites silences cryptic splice sites.
At repeats DNA methylation silences repeats. 

 

DNA methylation of intergenic regions and repeats is disrupted by a genome wide hypomethylation. This is one of the first effects of cancer on the genome.It progresses with increased tumorigenicity and is most common at the repeats.

Hypomethylation at intergenic regions and repeats results in genetic instability characterized by  euchromatic configuration.
As a result  repeats are activated and are susceptible to illegitimate recombination of repeats between different chromosomes transposition between different chromosomes  ,chromosomal deletions and insertions at repeats and aberrant chromosomal duplication  .
Activation of cryptic promoters and disruption to neighboring genes as well as within genes  occurs at intergenic regions...Activating repeats allows transcriptional interference from strong promoters.It prevents the mutation of repeats (MeC to T) by demethylating MeC  which when present , helps prevent transposition. 

This chromosomal instability driven by DNA demethylation appears to enhance tumorigenicity. Tumorigeniciy increases as demethylation proceeds. Increased tumorigenicity may be measured as a function of the amount of hypomethylation present.
Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
Paternal allele methylation blocks CTCF insulator protein from binding to the ICR of the Igf2 gene .
spreading methylation to the H19 gene promoter  blocking it's access to nearby enhancers. This allows enhancers to access the Igf2 gene and it's resultant expression.

Maternal allele methylation does not occur at the ICR to Igf2 allowing CTCF insulating protein to bind to it preventing local enhancers access to Igf2preventing it's expression.The enhancers target H19 instead and it is expressed .

Wilm's tumor is the result of double paternal imprinting  doubling the expression of Igf2 .
This is compounded by  maternal unmethylated ICR Kcnq1 in the same gene cluster that codes  an antisense lncRNA 
which blocks expression( recruits G9a and PRC2 that perform H3K9me and H3K27me silencing in cis)  of Cdkn1c a kinase growth inhibitor and cell cycle regulator .
Kcnq1 is normally methylated allowing Cdkn1c expression in the maternal allele.

Cancer results from double expression of growth promoting oncogene Igf2 and double silencing of Cdkn1c kinase growth inhibitor/cell cycle regulator.
Increased oncogene expression,lacking apoptosis, lacking growth inhibition ,lacking tumor suppression and cell cycle regulation leads  to unfettered growth and uncontrolled replication and immortality giving this cell line a selective advantage leading to cancer.

Decitabine is a DNA-mehtyltransferase inhibitor.

Decitabine inhibits aberrant epigenetically heritable propagation of DNA methylation at tumor suppressor gene promoters.
Incorporated into cellular DNA during replication and RNA during transcription as a citidine analogue .Inhibiting methyltransferase
 causing demethylation of the sequence  allowing cellular differentiation and apoptosis. Ref:(http://www.drugbank.ca/drugs/DB01262)

Over time Decitabine restores  normal unmethylated status of tumor gene suppressor promoters. 
Blocking the cancer induced methylation of these sites  carried forth (heritable) in future cell divisions
reactivating  tumor suppressor genes in these cell lines.Tumor suppressors regulate and slow cell division,repair DNA,

uncontrolled cell division ,lack of DNA repair  proceeding unrecognized and uncorrected (normally a candidate for apoptosis)

A sensitive period is a period in which epigenetic reprogramming is occurring.
Cells undergoing this process are highly susceptible to environmental factors that may prevent or change normal epigenetic controll
Abnormal phenotypes among the affected cell lines may result.

Sensitive periods:Cancer cells divide more rapidly often as a result, their cells are more sensitive at this time to certain agents vs. normal cell lines.
In this course sensitive periods refer to early embryonic pre-implantation and primordial germ cell(mid gestation)  re-establishment/reprogramming   epigenetic marks.
Included : early childhood and puberty, periods of rapid growth and development(pubertal germ cell maturation i.e. further re-establishment of epigenetic marks ) .
The 2-3 month period prior to fertilization in females is also a sensitive period . The soon to be released ova are finalizing their epigentic marks.

Treatment during sensitive periods with epigenetic modifying drugs risks altering the normal epigenetic marks being  re-established resulting in heritable genetic instability in normal cell lines predisposing them to dysfunctional phenotypes   including cancer.
