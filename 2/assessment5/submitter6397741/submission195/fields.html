  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
Decitabine a chemical analogue of cytidine. When it incorporated in the DNA or RNA, it inhibits DNA methytransferase. DNA cannot be methylated at the position occupied by Decitabine resulting in reduction in DNA methylation.

Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

There are two DNA methylation phenomenon seen in cancer, locus specific DNA hyper methylation and genome wide DNA hypomethylation. These DNA methylation alteration will result in genetic and epigenetic error accumulation leading to transformation of a normal cell into an invasive or metastatic tumor.

In normal cells, CpG islands are not methylated. In cancer cells, CpG islands of single gene or a set of genes and CpG islands shores of tumor suppressor genes are hypermethylated. The unusual hypermethylation of CpG islands is termed CIMPs. CIMPs are found to be associated with different types of cancer. These tumor suppressor genes, such as RB, MLH1, BRCA1, involve in cell cycle control, DNA damage repair etc.. When CpG islands and CpG island shores proceeding and/or around tumor suppressor genes are hypermethylated, the tumor suppressor genes become silent. Damaged DNA will not get corrected efficiently. Thus genomic instability increase which lead to uncontrolled tumor genesis and cancer.
ICRs hypermethylation clould lead to loss of imprinting which lead to overexpression of growth promoting genes thus resulting in cancer.

In normal cells, intergenic regions and repetitive elements are heavily methylated. While in cancer cells, these regions become hypomethylated. Disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements allow recombination and transposition occur resulting in genomic instability. The illegitimate recombination between repeats lead to loss of function due to large piece of DNA missing or generation of fusion DNA. It can also activate cryptic promoters and disrupt neighboring genes.
CpG poor promoters of oncogene (i.e. R-RAS) or gene that targets tumor suppressor gene (i.e. miR21) when not methylated become activated and lead to overexpression and uncontrolled cell growth.


Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
Igf2 and H19 cluster are separated with Imprint control region (ICR) which is methylated on paternal allele and not methylated on maternal allele. In normal cell, CTCF binds to the unmethylated ICR which is on the maternal allele and the enhancer can bind to H19 enable its expression and silence the Igf2 expression. On the paternal allele, due to methylation on the ICR and the promoter of H19, CTCF cannot bind to ICR, thus enhancer binds to Igf2 enable its expression while H19 is silenced. Loss of imprinting occurs in Wilm's tumor with both maternal and paternal allele methylated at ICRs. Expression of Igf2 are from both maternal and paternal alleles resulting in double dose of Igf2. Igf2 is growth promoting gene. Its increase in expression leads to cancer.
Disrupting imprinting at H19/Igf2 cluster, hypermethylation of ICRs, result in loss of growth suppress gene and overexpression of growth promoting gene, which leads to cancer.

  • Describe how altering DNA methylation can have enduring effects on the epigenome.
  • Define what is meant by a sensitive period.
  • Identify sensitive periods of development.

Epigenetic state is mitotically heritable. The methylation state of DNA is maintained through development. Thus once DNA methylation state is established, it remains unchanged and is passed to the daughter cell and so on. This is also the reason why once DNA methylation state is altered, it has the life long enduring effects on the epigenome.

The sensitive period is the time when epigenetic mark is in reprogramming during germ cells formation and early development of embyo, fetus. Thus prenatal and early postnatal development stages are sensitive periods. Young children especially at the age of 9-12 for boy and 8-11 for girls when they go through purberty,  women before pregnant, during pregnant and nursing are in sensitive period as well.

Treating patients during sensitive periods will affect epigenetic stage on not only the tumor cells but also normal cells. Changing normal cells epigenetic marks will have adverse effect for the patients' life. Thus it is inadvisable and should be avoid.
