  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
DNA methylation is about adding a methyl group to the cytosine or adenine nucleotides. It can result in transcriptional silencing at CpG islands by blocking the binding of transcription factors or recruting MBD proteins.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
DNA hypermethylation of CpG islands is usually found in cancer. In other words, enriched DNA methylation is found.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
 The DNA hypermethylation may silence tumour suppressor genes in cancer. 
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
DNA methylation in Intergenic regions and repetitive elements can result in genomic stability.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
DNA hypoimethylation of intergenic regions and repetitive elements is usually found in cancer. In other words, depleted DNA methylation is found.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.
DNA hypoimethylation of intergenic regions and repetitive elements cam result in genomic instability e.g. illegitimate recombination between repeats, activation of repeats and transposition, activation of cryptic promoters and disruption to neighboring genes.
