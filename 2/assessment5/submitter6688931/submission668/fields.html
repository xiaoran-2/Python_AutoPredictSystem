Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

In a cancer cell there is DNA hypermethylation of CpG islands which are found on the promoters of tumor suppressor genes while repetitive elements, intergenic regions and the introns of genes remain hypomethylated throughout the genome. Hypomethylation lead to loosely packaged chromatin of repetitive elements and intergenic regions and that contributes to illegitimate recombination. Transposition of repeats disrupts an expression of neighboring genes if they happen to jump and become a cryptic promoter. In addition you get insertion and deletions due to genomic instability as a consequence of hypomethylation of repetitive elements and inergenic regions. Hypermethylation of CpG islands is associated with a tumor suppressor genes. And when the silencing of tumor suppressor genes occurs – the cell’s growth becomes unstoppable. 

In contrast, in a normal cell repetitive elements, intergeninc regions and the introns of genes are methylated and CpG islands are not.
