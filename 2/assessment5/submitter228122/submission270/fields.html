  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation in normal and cancer is almost reciprocal situation. In normal cells intergenic and repetitive regions are methylated & CpG islands are free of methylation, whereas in cancer cells CpG islands are methylated & repetitive and intergenic regions are un-methylated. As repetitive and intergenic regions contributes large portion of genome this leads to genome wide hypomethylation and local (CpG island)  hypermethylation, utlimately landing to genomic instability and silencing of tumor suppressor genes which promotes cancer. As methylation is mitotically heritable tumor suppressor gene remain silent generation to generation, promoting cancer. demetylation of repetitive and intergenic regions causes illegitimate recombination which produces abnormal karyotypes.
