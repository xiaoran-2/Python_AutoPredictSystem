  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.

Normally, the function at CpG islands of DNA methylation is to silence the genes. The basic idea is when you have cancer, the genes wiil be hypermethylated at tumor-suppressor genes and hypomethylated at other genes included oncogenes, so the suppressor is silenced while the oncogene is activated. The consequences of disrupting DNA methylation at CpG islands are the oncogenes can become hyperplasia then cancer because they are hyper-regulated and also their suppressor genes were "suppressed". DNA methylation also affect on ICRs: on one hand, they decrease expression of tumor- restricting genes, on the other hand, they overwhelming our body with genes that promote growth.
As for intergenic regions and repetitive elements, DNA methylation is usually occurs. They keep the genes, more specific, the chromosomes from reconstruction or recombination. In cancer, these regions are hypomethylated, so the chromosomes are more fragile and unstable, When there are problems with DNA hypomethylation in those regions, the chromosomes are not densed anymore, they move around or be deleted etc. The result is the tumor have chance to grow when you have problems with your genome, as well as the oncogenes are enhanced and the suppressors are no longer around.
