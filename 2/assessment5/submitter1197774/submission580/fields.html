Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

In cancer cells there are two type of altered methylation. It can either be locus specific hypermethylation (inactivation of tumor suppressors) and genom-wide hypomethylation (which makes open repetitive regions for transciption) and these cases occurs usually at the same time. Hypermethylated CGIs differ by tumor type.
In normal cells CpG islands tend to be hypomethylated while repetiteive elements, intergenic regions and introns are methylated. Cancer cells are the opposite. DNA methylation is mitotically heritable so inactivation of tumor suppressors can spread rapidly.
Hypomethylation of intergenic regions and repetitive elements cause genomic instability. This can occur in effects like deletions from the genom, translocations of the repetitive elements (which can distrupt neighbouring genes) or insertions. We know this occufrs from either human diseases (i.e. mutations in DNMT3B) and from most human cancers.
Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
At the H19/Igf2 cluster the Imprinting region is normally unmethylated on the maternal allele and methylated on the paternal allele. DNA methylation at the ICR spread downstream. So the H19 lnRNA is expressed only from the maternal allele. This is a reservoir for the micro RNA which is an enhancer for Igf2. If the imprinting region is unmathylated it will be bound by CTCF protein which blocks enhancing the Igf2.
If the maternal allele got methylated (i.e. imprinting disorders) it will act like a second paternal allele which will lead to upregulation of Igf2 which is an oncogene, and will cause the shut down of the Cdkn1c gene which is a tumor supressor. This uniparental disomy can cause Beckwith Wiedemann syndrome and it can cause childhood's tumour like Wilm's tumour in the kidney.
Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
Decitabine is an DNMT inhibitor. It's used in myeloplastic syndrome which is precursor of AML (acute myelogenous leukemia). In tumour-cells the tumour spuressor genes are inactiveted by methylation of these genes. By using DNMT inhibitors (i.e. Decitabine) after mitosis in the daughter these regions will be unmethylated (as in normal cells), so the tumour suressor gene will be active again.
