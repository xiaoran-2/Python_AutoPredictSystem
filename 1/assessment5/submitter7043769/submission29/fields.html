Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

The normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands is to silence the gene expression. For instance, a female X chromosome which is inactivated is methylated at CpG islands so that male and female have the same number of active X chromosome.
However, DNA hypermethylation of CpG islands can also silences tumour suppressor genes which prevent cancer. If it occurs, the normal tissue will be growth unusually. Then, the tumour will be formed.
Since tumour maybe the symbol of cancer, DNA hypermethylation of CpG islands, which silences tumour suppressor genes, contributes to cancer.
The role of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is to silence some genes that cause instability. For example, cells without mathylated will cause genomic instability.
If DNA hypomethylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer, chromosomes in the nucleus of a eukaryotic cell will be different than normal. For example, some type of chromosomes will increase in number and some types of chromosomes will be damaged.
Since chromosomal instability cause depletion of DNA methylation which enhance tumorigenesis, DNA hypomethylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.
Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
In paternal allele, the methylation of imprint control region prevent combination of CTCF, which is the insulator protein,and thus the enhancers usually increase Igf2 expression.
In maternal allele, the imprint control region is not methylated. CTCF combine with the imprint control region. Moreover, enchancers, which cannot promote Igf2 expression due to CTCF blocking, promote H19 expression instead and thus silence Igf2 expression.
Lose of imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster lead to methylation of imprint control region at maternal allele. That increases Igf2 expression and hence form Wilm’s tumour.
Imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster can cause more expression on growth promoting genes and less expression on growth restricting genesis. These event is usually seen before cancer.

Altering DNA methylation will cause epigenetic change on daughter and granddaughter cells. As a result, enduring effects occur on the epigenome.
Sensitive period is the period of create epigenetic marks.
The periods from pre-implantation development to germ cell development are sensitive periods of development.
