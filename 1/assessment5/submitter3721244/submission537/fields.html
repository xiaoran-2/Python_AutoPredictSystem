  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

CpG islands are usually kept free of methylation independent of their activity state. For example, a CpG island near of tumour suppressor gene will be unmethylated, and the gene could be transcript. In cancer, the methylation of CpG cause the silencing of the tumour suppressor gene.The methylation of the promoter CpG islands of the tumour suppressor genes cause the lost of the control of the cell cycle, apoptosis or DNA repair.

On the one hand Intergenic regions and repetitive elements are usually methylated in normal cells, on the other hand, in cancer these regions are unmethylated. This could cause genomic instability and deregulation of tissue specific and imprinted genes and silencing of tumour suppressor genes.


Q2. Describe how disruption of imprinting can contribute to cancer, using the example of the H19/Igf2 cluster. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the methylation pattern of the paternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe the methylation pattern of the maternal allele and how this determines Igf2 expression status. 
  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

On one hand, in normal cells, as it is shown in the picture of the cluster, Igf2 is expressed only in the paternal allele while H19 is only expressed in the maternal allele. This is happen because H19 is methylated on the paternal allele. The enhancer of the paternal allele encourage of the Igf2. However, the enhancers of the maternal allele, due to the union of the molecule CTCF to the ICR, encourage the expression of H19.

On the other hand, in Wilm’s tumour, Igf2 is expressed in both alleles. Due to DNA methylation of the ICR of the maternal allele. This overexpression of Igf2 is the cause of the Wilm’s tumour, because Igf2 is growth promoting. And it is an example of loss imprinting.
