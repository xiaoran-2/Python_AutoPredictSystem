Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

DNA methylation at CpG islands is an inactive epigenetic mark,associated with silencing of genes and also involved in X chromosome inactivation. Inheritable property of DNA methylation ensures the genomic stability.
 In a cancer cell CpG islands found in promoter regions are more likely to be hypermethylated than in normal cell.
The hypermethylation at CpG islands in promoter region resulted in silencing of tumor suppressing genes,controlling of cell cycle,apoptosis and DNA repair mechanisms which resulted in conversion on normal cell into cancer cell,metastasis and immortality of cells.
Hypomethylation of CpG poor promoters are associated with activation of potent oncogene R-RAS in gastric cancer and miR21 which results in glioma.
The function of DNA methylation at intergenic regions is to maintain genomic integrity.
 The function of DNA methylation at repetitive elements is to maintain genomic stability by carrying out silencing of repeats to prevent transposition and transcriptional interference from strong promoters.It may also prevents illegitimate recombination.
Intergenic regions and repetitive elements in a cancer cell found to be hypomethylated.In normal cell intergenic regions and repetitive are methylated.
Hypomethylation of repetitive elements can cause the illegitimate recombination of chromosomes which shares the genetically similar regions.The repeats can also be activated because of hypomethylation may results into activation of neighboring genes.  
Hypomethylation at intergenic regions cause insertion,deletion or reciprocal translocation of chromosomes.
In general hypomethylation of repetitive elements/intergenic regions can cause genomic instability and cancer.

  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
On the paternal allele imprint control region in H19/Igf2 gene is methylated.CTCF can no longer bind and CTCF is not binding and there is no insulator action then the enhancers are free to act on Igf2 and promote Igf2 expresion from paternal allele.
On the maternal allele imprint control region in H19/Igf2 unmethylated. So when this imprint control is unmethylated it is bound by insulator protein called CTCF results in inactivation of Igf2 gene expression.
In uniparental disomy,you have two paternal like alleles and loss of maternal like allele.That means you get too much Igf2 gene expression which in fact is growth promoting gene and you will get no Cdkn1c expression which is tumor suppressing gene. And that's how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
