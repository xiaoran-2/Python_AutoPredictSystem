  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

RNA polymerase starts transcribing DNA all around in a a randomly way. Once the cell has finite resources, a non methylated DNA would consume a lot of time and energy from the cell, transcribing unwanted and possible harmful random DNA. The methylation at CpG islands form a 5-methylcytosine changing the expression and therefore knock out that possible garbage located in intergenic regions and repetitive elements on the DNA.
In cancer cells, has been found that the balance is disrupted having a hypermethylation of tumor suppressor genes and hypomethylation of oncogenes witch could promote mitotic recombination and chromosome rearrangement. The balance is lost, the cell replicate indefinitely and the original methylation configuration is lost resulting in more disrupted DNA.

In a normal person, IGF2 is active on paternal and inactive on maternal but in Wilms’tumour the maternal also express IGF2 resulting in a double dosage.
In maternal, CTCF is unmethylated, alowing it to bind to the insulator, this allow enhancers to act on H19 resulting in a silent IGF2. In paternal, CTCF is methylated, therefore enhancer can act on IGF2 resulting in expression.
