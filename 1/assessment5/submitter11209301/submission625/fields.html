Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

CPG islands are promoting regions for cancer suppression genes, therefore play a very important role in cancer prevention. However, as a person age (together with other factors) these CpG islands including their surrounds / "shores" become methylated. This is in contrast to the rest of the genome including the inter genetic region and repetitive elements which becomes less methylated or hypomethylated over the same period of time. The more methylated the CpG islands become, the less likely are they expressed, therefore the less capable they become to do their cancer suppression job. This may lead to cancer.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
Decitabine, better known as Dacogen, is a DNA demythelation agent. It functions by preventing further methylation of CpG islands or regions where genes that are involved in cancer suppression and cell death programming. This enable the cells to regain control over cell growth, therefore to prevent further tumor growth and metastasis.

How can drugs that alter DNA methylation have effects that last beyond the period of drug treatment? Discuss whether there are any periods of development when you would avoid treating patients with such drugs. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe how altering DNA methylation can have enduring effects on the epigenome.
  • Define what is meant by a sensitive period.
  • Identify sensitive periods of development.
  • Explain why treating patients during sensitive periods would be inadvisable.
DNA methylation are mitotically inheritable. However, of more importance is the fact that methylation and other relevant epicene tic markers may be inheritable during meiosis and in other mechanisms involved in inter generational reprogramming in sperm and egg cell formation. If the appropriate epicene tic marks are not in these important steps removed or re programmed, inappropriate epicene tic traits will be inherited, and proper formation of undifferentiated stem cells will not fully occur. Sensitive periods are those periods that such important reprogramming occur e.g. During sperm and egg cell formation, and early embryonic development, and critical periods in early cell differentiation in the fetus.
