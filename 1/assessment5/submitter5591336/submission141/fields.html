  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.

DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted by age and environment.

The normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is that both are silenced.
DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer by age and environment.
DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer because it causes genomic instability.

  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.
The methylation of the paternal ICR results in not binding of CTCF so the enhancers can acces Lgf2 promotor thus Lgf2 is expressed, the methylation of the ICR results also in methylation of H19 promotor so H19 is not expressed.
The ICR of the maternal allele is not methylated thus CTCF can bind on it and it blocks the enhancers from accessing the Igf2 promotor and they instead promote H19 expression.
In Wilm’s tumour is caused by a hypermethilation of the maternal ICR so CTCF cannot bind on the ICR so the enhancers can acces Lgf2 promotor thus Lgf2 is expressed, the methylation of the ICR results also in methylation of H19 promotor so H19 is not expressed.this results in a paternal expression AND a maternal expression of Igf2 (this is a growth factor and to much of it contributes to cancer).
Disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer because Igf2 is a growth factor and to much of it contributes to cancer and H19 is an enhancer blocker and to little of it contributes also to cancer.
