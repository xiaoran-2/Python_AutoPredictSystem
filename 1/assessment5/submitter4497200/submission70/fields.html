Q1. Describe how DNA methylation is altered in cancer. In your answer include the following points:

  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation at CpG islands.
  • Describe how DNA methylation of CpG islands is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation at CpG islands contributes to cancer.
  • Describe the normal function of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements.
  • Describe how DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements is disrupted in cancer.
  • Explain how disruption of DNA methylation in intergenic regions and repetitive elements contributes to cancer.

CpG islands are generally unmethylated, but methylation of CpG islands is almost always associated with silencing the genes that upstream of itself.  In cancer, CpG islands that are not supposed to be silenced may become methylated, leading to hypermethylation of CpG islands.  Once this happens, activation, lack of activaion, transcription and replication errors will occur, usually leading to the uncontrolled replication.  DNA methylation at intergeneic regions and IAPs limits the process of transcription in the locations where it should not be occurring.  In cancer, they become demethylated, leading to the transcription of regions that should not have been active.  DNA can be transcribed at random and with potentially bad results.

Q3. The Economist article “Cancer’s epicentre” describes several drugs that affect epigenetic processes. Explain how Decitabine may be used to treat cancer, with reference to effects on the epigenome. In your answer include the following points:

  • Identify the class of epigenetic inhibitors that Decitabine belongs to. 
  • Describe the impact of Decitabine on DNA methylation.
  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
The drug known as Decitabine works on the enzyme EZH2.  Per the article, a mutation causes EZH2 to become overactive.  This has the effect of methylating more histones than it should and silencing genes that should not be silenced, including tumor-suppressing genes.  Decitabine acts to inhibit the overactivity of EZH2, reducing the overmethylation caused by EZH2.  Decitabine also had the effect of reducing the spreading of tumor cells.  Decitabine was also described as having no apparent effect on nearby healthy cells.
