  • Describe how imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster is disrupted in Wilm’s tumour. 
  • Explain how disrupting imprinting at the H19/Igf2 cluster contributes to cancer.

1.
CTCF insulates Igf2 from downstream enhancers. 
  • DNA methylation at ICR blocks binding of CTCF binding.
  • Without CTCF, DNA methylation spreads to H19 promoter to silence and enhancers can access Igf2 to activate .
  • The ICR at IGF2 and H19 is normally methylated on the paternal allele. Methylation is excluded from the maternal allele by CTCF binding. The presence of CTCF confers an insulator function upon the ICR, which blocks access of the IGF2 promoters to the enhancers downstream of the H19 gene.
1+2.
  • Paternal allele: Enhancers help express igf-2 as ICR is methylated and CTCF can't bind.
  • Maternal allele: No methylation at ICR, CTCF binds ICR and blocks transcription.
3.

Imprinting of the insulin-2 and insulin-like growth factor 2 genes, can be disrupted by maternal inheritance of a targeted deletion of the H19 gene and its flanking sequence.Paternal inheritance of the disruption has no effect, which presumably reflects the normally silent state of the paternal gene.

  • Describe how Decitabine can have an anti-tumour effect. 
1.
Hypomethylating agent/ cytidine analog
2.
It hypomethylates DNA by inhibiting DNA methyltransferase.It can only be incorporated into DNA strands but not RNA chains.
3.
a.) Hypomethylating agents block the activity of DNA methyltransferase (DNA methyltransferase inhibitors / DNMT inhibitors). DNA methylation affects cellular function through successive generations of cells without changing the underlying DNA sequence.
b.) DNA methylation is the modification of DNA nucleotides by addition of a methyl group. These methyl groups are associated with changes in the ability of the corresponding DNA to be used. Patterns of DNA methylation are stable during cellular division. Excessive methylation of TSG's in some cancers contributes to the growth and survival of the cancer. c.)  Its incorporation into DNA leads to a covalent binding with DNA methyltransferases, which prevents DNA synthesis and subsequent cytotoxicity
